[h1]Troubleshooting USB Connection and Contention Issues[/h1]

This article explains a class of USB connection issues that can affect devices connected over USB. While it is commonly described as a USB "bandwidth" issue, it is more accurately a USB scheduling or contention issue. The troubleshooting below can help determine whether this is the cause of the behavior you're seeing.

[h2]Common symptoms[/h2]

You may be experiencing this issue if a USB device (for example, a Steam Controller Puck) behaves differently depending on which USB port it is connected to, or if it works correctly on one computer but not another.

Other symptoms may include:

[list]

[*]The device intermittently disconnects.

[*]The device fails to connect reliably.

[*]Performance, responsiveness, or latency changes depending on the USB port being used.

[*]The issue changes after connecting or disconnecting other USB devices.

[/list]

[url id=troubleshooting][/url]

[h2]Troubleshooting[/h2]

If this sounds like something you may be encountering, try the following:

[olist]

[*]Try a different USB port on your computer.

[*]If another USB port is not available, connect the USB device to a USB hub. While this may not seem intuitive, it helps determine whether the issue is related to USB scheduling.

[*]Disconnect as many other USB devices as practical and test again.

[*]If that resolves the issue, reconnect the other USB devices one at a time to identify which device is contributing to the contention.

[*]If possible, test the USB device on another computer to determine whether the behavior follows the device or remains with the original system.

[/olist]

Devices that are more likely to contribute to USB contention include:

[list]

[*]USB audio devices (gaming headsets, DACs, sound cards)

[*]High-performance or high polling rate gaming peripherals

[*]Additional Steam Controller Pucks

[*]Other wireless USB adapters or dongles

[/list]

Moving both the affected USB device and other USB devices to different USB ports may resolve the issue.

[url id=puck][/url]

[h2]Steam Controller Puck-specific information[/h2]

The Steam Controller Puck is more likely than many USB devices to expose USB scheduling or contention issues because it supports up to four controllers simultaneously using a low-latency wireless connection.

As a result, some USB controllers or USB hubs may experience scheduling contention when other Full-Speed USB devices are connected to the same USB controller or hub.

Changing USB ports, using a different hub, or rearranging other USB devices can change how devices are grouped internally, which is why these troubleshooting steps often resolve the issue.
